# MCC USB-2637 vs USB-2627

Direct spec-sheet comparison of the two boards from the USB-2600 series
relevant to PIONER. Sources:
[specs/USB-2637.pdf](../specs/USB-2637.pdf),
[specs/USB-2627.pdf](../specs/USB-2627.pdf),
[specs/USB-2600-Series-data.pdf](../specs/USB-2600-Series-data.pdf).

PIONER currently runs on **USB-2637** (committed in `config.json`,
`InputMode: 2 = SINGLE_ENDED`; see [../README.md](../README.md) and
[../design_notes.md](../design_notes.md)). USB-2627 is the smaller sibling.

---

## Bottom line

**Three things differ. Everything else is identical.** The two boards
are the same silicon with a different number of analog-input front
ends and one extra ribbon-cable header on the larger one. For
PIONER's current workload (6 AI channels at 20 kHz, 2 AO channels) the
two boards are functionally interchangeable -- you'd only feel the
difference if you ever scanned more than 16 channels with
per-channel ranges.

---

## What differs

| Parameter                 | USB-2627                           | USB-2637                           |
|---------------------------|------------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Analog inputs (SE)**    | **16**                             | **64**                             |
| **Channel queue length**  | up to **16** elements              | up to **64** elements              |
| **40-pin header connectors** | 3 (J2, J4, J5)                  | 4 (J2, **J3**, J4, J5) -- J3 carries the extra 48 SE inputs |

The three differences are all aspects of the same architectural choice:
USB-2637 wires 4x more SE front-end channels to the same backend ADC.
Channel queue capacity scales with channel count. The extra physical
inputs need an extra ribbon-cable header to reach the screw-terminal
board.

Cabling note: TB-100 / TB-103 / CIO-MINI40 screw terminal boards work
with both. On USB-2627 the breakout uses 3 headers, on USB-2637 it uses
4 -- you just need one more C40FF-x cable for the extra header.

## What is identical

Everything below is **identical** on both boards (verified against
chapters 4-5 of each user's guide).

### Analog input front end

- A/D converter: **16-bit successive approximation**
- Input voltage range: **+/-10 V** (fixed)
- Absolute maximum input voltage: +/-25 V (power on), +/-10.5 V (power off)
- Input impedance: 1 GOhm (power on), 390 Ohm (power off)
- Input bias current: +/-100 pA
- Input bandwidth (-3 dB, small signal): 3.1 MHz
- Input capacitance: 40 pF
- Maximum working voltage: +/-10.1 V relative to AGND
- Crosstalk (adjacent channels, DC to 10 kHz): -80 dB
- Input coupling: DC
- Mode: **single-ended only.** Neither board has a differential mode
  (relevant only because earlier docs/code assumed one existed -- see
  [../postmortem/2026-05-23-differential-vs-defaultio.md](../postmortem/2026-05-23-differential-vs-defaultio.md)).

### Sampling

- Sampling rate: 0.0149 Hz to 1,000 kHz, software selectable
- Hardware-paced throughput: **1 MS/s max** (system dependent)
- Software-paced throughput: 33 to 4,000 S/s typ
- Burst mode: burst rate 1 us, software selectable
- Warm-up time: 15 min min

### Accuracy (DC, +/-10 V range)

- Gain error: 0.031 % of reading
- Offset error: 915 uV
- INL error: 0.0076 % of range
- Absolute accuracy at full scale: 4775 uV
- Noise: 8 counts peak-to-peak, 1.21 LSBrms (32k samples at max rate)
- Settling time (10 V step, multichannel):
  - 1 us settling: 0.0152 % FSR
  - 5 us: 0.0061 % FSR
  - 10 us: 0.0015 % FSR

### Analog output (XDACx)

- **4 channels**, **16-bit**, **+/-10 V** range, calibrated
- Output update rate: **1 MS/s max** (note: update rate is not affected by
  the number of channels in the scan)
- DAC settling time to rated accuracy on 10 V step: 2 us
- Slew rate: 20 V/us
- Differential non-linearity: +/-0.25 LSB typ, +/-1 LSB max
- Output current: +/-3.5 mA max
- Output short-circuit protection: unlimited duration (XDACx connected to AGND)
- Power-on / reset state: DACs cleared to zero-scale: 0 V, +/-150 mV
- Pacer source: two programmable sources -- internal output scan
  clock or external (**XDPCR**), independent of the AI external clock
  (**XAPCR**)
- Trigger sources: TTLTRG
- Calibrated absolute accuracy: 16.0 LSB
- AO calibration procedure: internally routed to AI for self-calibration

### Trigger

- Trigger source: **TTLTRG**
- Trigger mode: software-programmable, edge sensitive (rising/falling) or
  level sensitive (high/low). Default: edge sensitive, rising edge.
- Trigger latency: **1 us + 1 clock cycle** max
- Trigger pulse width: 100 ns min
- Input: 33 Ohm series resistor, 49.9 kOhm pull-down to GND
- High voltage: 2.2 V min, 5.5 V absolute max
- Low voltage: 1.5 V max, -0.5 V absolute min

### External clock I/O

- Terminal names: **XAPCR** (AI), **XDPCR** (AO)
- Both inputs, active on rising edge
- Receive pacer clock from external source
- Input clock rate: 1 MHz max
- Clock pulse width: 100 ns min
- **The two pacer clocks are independent.** There is no internal shared
  pacer that drives both AO and AI; if you need clock-level synchrony
  between AO and AI you must wire one externally to the other (see
  [../design_notes.md](../design_notes.md) Section "Hardware trigger").

### Digital I/O

- 24 TTL-level lines, three banks of 8
- Each bit configurable as input (power-on default) or output
- Pull-up/pull-down: 47 kOhm, jumper-selectable (default: pull-down)
- Transfer rate (system-paced, async): 33 to 4,000 reads/writes per second typ
- Input high: 2.0 V min (5.0 V absolute max); low: 0.8 V max
- Output high: 4.4 V min @ -50 uA; low: 0.1 V max @ 50 uA
- Output current: 60 mA total max (24 mA per pin max; 2.5 mA each with all 24 bits enabled)

### Counters

- 4 channels, 32-bit, event counter type
- Terminals: CNT0..CNT3
- Input source: 68-pin SCSI (pins 5, 39, 4, 38) and 40-pin J4 (pins 35, 34, 37, 36)
- Input frequency: 20 MHz max
- High/low pulse width: 25 ns min
- Read/write rates (software paced): 33 to 8,000 per second

### Timer outputs

- 4 channels (TMR0..TMR3), PWM
- Internal clock: 64 MHz; output rate = 64 MHz / 2^32, software selectable
- High/low pulse width: 10.42 ns min
- Register widths: 32-bit
- Square wave output

### Memory

- **Data FIFO: 4 kS analog input, 2 kS analog output** (same on both boards)
- Non-volatile memory: 32 KB (30 KB firmware, 2 KB calibration / user data)

### USB

- USB 2.0 (high-speed), backward compatible with USB 1.1
- Cable: A-B type, min 24 AWG VBUS/GND, min 28 AWG D+/D-; 3 m max

### Power

- Quiescent supply current: 360 mA
- USB-powered, no external supply

### Mechanical & environmental

- PCB dimensions: 152.4 x 150.62 mm (6.00 x 5.93 in)
- Operating temperature: 0 to 55 deg C
- Storage temperature: -40 to 85 deg C
- Humidity: 0-90 % non-condensing

### Connectors and breakouts

- Main: P1 = 68-pin SCSI Type III female connector
- USB-2627: J2, J4, J5 (three 40-pin headers; AMP# 2-103328-0)
- USB-2637: J2, **J3**, J4, J5 (four 40-pin headers; AMP# 2-103328-0) --
  the extra J3 routes the additional 48 SE inputs
- Compatible terminal boards on both: **TB-100**, **TB-103**, **CIO-MINI40**
- P1 ribbon cable: CA-68-3R (3 ft); 40-pin: C40FF-x

## Implications for PIONER

We currently use:
- **6 AI channels** (Uref, Umod, Uhtr, Uaux, Utpl, Uhtrabs)
- **2 AO channels** primarily (heater drive ch1 + modulation ch0; ch2 reserved for trigger)
- **AI rate 20 kHz**, AO rate matches AI (production config)

That places us very far inside both boards' envelope:

| Constraint                               | USB-2627 limit | USB-2637 limit | Our use      | Headroom         |
|------------------------------------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|------------------|
| AI channels                              | 16 SE          | 64 SE          | 6            | 10x / 11x        |
| AI sample rate                           | 1 MS/s         | 1 MS/s         | 20 kHz       | 50x              |
| AI aggregate throughput (6 ch x 20 kHz)  | 1 MS/s         | 1 MS/s         | 120 kS/s     | ~8x              |
| Channel queue (per-channel ranges)       | 16 elements    | 64 elements    | 6 elements   | 2.6x / 10x       |
| AO channels                              | 4              | 4              | 2-3          | 1.3-2x           |
| AO update rate                           | 1 MS/s         | 1 MS/s         | 20 kHz       | 50x              |
| Data FIFO (AI)                           | 4 kS           | 4 kS           | -            | see below        |

### Where the boards would actually differ for us

- **Nowhere**, on the current workload. Both boards are oversized for it.
- If we ever wanted to expand to 17+ AI channels (e.g., adding a thermal
  imaging strip, or scanning a guard ring on the chip), USB-2637 keeps
  going; USB-2627 maxes out at 16. Realistic for PIONER? Probably not.
- If we ever wanted to load >16 different per-channel ranges into the AI
  queue (for a non-trivial autogain strategy across many channels),
  USB-2637 supports 64-element queues; USB-2627 supports 16. Again, not
  the current workload.

### FIFO depth and OVERRUN margin

Both boards have a **4 kS AI FIFO**. At our typical rate of 6 channels
x 20 kHz = 120 kS/s aggregate, the FIFO fills in **4096 / 120000 ~ 34 ms**.
That is the absolute upper bound on how long the host can stall (GC,
disk write, VM scheduler hiccup) before `ULError.OVERRUN` triggers. See
[../known-issues.md](../known-issues.md) Section 1 for the OVERRUN
incident and how the IR branch's switch to `ScanOption.DEFAULTIO`
mitigates it.

---

## Summary

- USB-2627 = 16 SE, 16-element channel queue, 3 ribbon headers.
- USB-2637 = 64 SE, 64-element channel queue, 4 ribbon headers.
- Everything else (ADC bits, sample rate, AO, triggers, clocks, FIFO,
  DIO, counters, timers, USB, power, mechanical) is **identical**.
- For PIONER as it stands today, the two boards are functionally
  interchangeable. We keep using USB-2637 because that's what is in
  the lab.
